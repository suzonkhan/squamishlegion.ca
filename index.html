Royal Canadian Legion Diamond Head Branch 277

Affectionately known as the Squamish Legion, we reside at 40194 Glenalder Place in the Garibaldi Highlands north of the town center of Squamish BC. We are 160 members strong and we have a passion for volunteering and raising funds for our community. One of our fund raisers is our weekly Meat Draw that happens in our upstairs lounge. Our lounge is a great place to meet with your friends and enjoy the view from the balcony. Our community fund raising focuses mainly on youth organizations as we see them as potential leaders for our community. We see the growth that is happening in our community and we want to be part of the future for Squamish. We celebrate legion Day the last Saturday in June each year and we invite all members of the community including youth, to see who were are and what we do. Very important to us are the veterans in our community - young and old - still serving and retired. We strive to connect with those who are serving and have served our country so that we may give any aid if required or comradeship with a one year free membership. We continue to raise funds for organizations that help veterans, such as the Veterans Transition Network that works with members of the forces who have PTSD. If you want to have a purpose that makes a difference join us. We welcome new members who have our passion for the Squamish Community. Continue browsing our website for further information on our Legion and drop by the lounge for a visit,

Royal Canadian Legion

The most sacrosanct and central element in Remembrance is the two minutes of silence. During this time of reflection, Canadians pause to honour, thank and Remember our Fallen. The Legion undertook a project to capture the solemnity and highlight the significance of this period of reflection through the production of a special video appropriately entitled: “Two Minutes of Silence.” The premise is simple, yet powerful; a compilation of Canadians, all of whom will be wearing the Poppy, pausing for a moment to Remember. The central part of this video is two minutes in duration and has no spoken words or music. In This Section Two Minutes of Silence
